LLM Wiki – example of an "idea file"  (296 pts, 95 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47640875

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the concept of an "LLM Wiki," a personal knowledge management system where a large language model (LLM) autonomously writes and maintains a wiki. The idea is to leverage LLMs to handle tasks like summarizing, cross-referencing, and organizing information, allowing users to focus on sourcing and exploring content rather than manual documentation.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and debate regarding the effectiveness and originality of the LLM Wiki concept. Some users argue that it could lead to "context pollution" and suggest using decision trees to manage information better. Others believe that the integration of a wiki can significantly reduce context bloat and enhance knowledge synthesis. There is also discussion about the relationship between this approach and existing concepts like Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), with some commenters asserting that the LLM Wiki is simply a variation of RAG, while others emphasize its unique aspects, such as the LLM's ability to maintain and update the knowledge base dynamically.

Notable insights include references to historical ideas about man-computer symbiosis and concerns about potential "model collapse" from compounding errors in LLM-generated content. Some users express excitement about the potential of LLM Wikis as a tool for personal knowledge management, while others remain skeptical about their practical utility and the risks involved. Overall, the discussion highlights both the innovative potential and the challenges of using LLMs for knowledge management.
