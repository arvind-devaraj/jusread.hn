Generative AI Vegetarianism  (40 pts, 55 comments)

The Hacker News discussion titled "Generative AI Vegetarianism" revolves around the concept of rejecting generative AI technologies in favor of human-created content, drawing an analogy to vegetarianism, which emphasizes abstaining from meat. The original post suggests that this stance could be labeled as "AI Vegetarianism," prompting a debate about the appropriateness and implications of such terminology.

In the comments, users express a mix of agreement and disagreement regarding the label. Some suggest alternative names like "organic software" or "slop-free," while others critique the term "vegetarianism" for its existing connotations and potential negative associations. There is also a discussion about the implications of labeling non-AI content as the default, with some commenters arguing that it frames the use of AI as the norm, which could undermine the value of human-created work.

Notable insights include concerns about the societal perception of those who reject AI, with some likening it to the stigma historically faced by vegetarians. Others emphasize the importance of finding a positive and clear label that reflects the values of those who prefer human-made content without invoking moral panic. The conversation highlights the broader anxieties around AI's impact on creativity and employment, as well as the desire for a more meaningful discourse around technology's role in society.
