https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47531922

The Hacker News discussion centers around a project called Sup AI, developed by Ken, a 20-year-old Stanford CS student, and his father Scott, an AI research scientist. Sup AI employs a confidence-weighted ensemble approach, running multiple AI models in parallel and synthesizing their outputs based on the confidence of their predictions. This method aims to reduce errors and hallucinations that individual models may produce. In evaluations on Humanity's Last Exam, Sup AI achieved a score of 52.15%, outperforming the best individual model by a statistically significant margin.

In the comments, users engage in discussions about the effectiveness of the entropy-weighting method, with some questioning whether the additional cost of running multiple models is justified by the performance gains. There are debates about the challenges of detecting hallucinations and the potential for AI models to self-correct. Some commenters express interest in trying Sup AI for specific use cases, while others suggest that more benchmarks would strengthen the project's credibility. Notably, Scott's involvement in clarifying technical aspects of the project sparked some light-hearted criticism about his need to correct his son's post publicly. Overall, the conversation reflects a mix of enthusiasm for the innovation and skepticism about its practical applications.
