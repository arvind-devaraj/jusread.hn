The coming industrialisation of exploit generation with LLMs  (265 pts, 170 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46676081

The Hacker News discussion revolves around an article titled "The coming industrialisation of exploit generation with LLMs," which explores the potential for large language models (LLMs) to automate the creation of software exploits. The author suggests that this could lead to a new era where organizations can generate exploits at scale, limited only by the computational resources they can allocate.

In the comments, there is a debate about the effectiveness of LLMs in generating useful exploits versus the prevalence of low-quality bug reports. Some users argue that both perspectives can coexist, emphasizing that the quality of output depends on the expertise of the individuals using the LLMs. Others highlight the challenges maintainers face when sifting through potentially irrelevant or incorrect reports generated by less skilled users. Notably, there is a consensus that while LLMs can be powerful tools, their effectiveness is heavily influenced by the skill level of the users and the setup of the environment in which they operate.

Overall, the discussion underscores a divide between those who see LLMs as a revolutionary tool for security research and those who caution against the risks of misuse and the potential for generating noise in the form of unverified reports. There is also a recognition of the need for proper methodologies and expertise to harness the full potential of LLMs in exploit generation.
