In the comments, users express a mix of skepticism and concern regarding Meta's intentions. Some argue that the company is trying to capture data to enhance its AI capabilities, while others believe that Meta already has sufficient public data from its social networks and doesn't need to invade privacy further. There is also a debate about the implications of AR/VR technology and the potential for privacy invasions, with some commenters advocating for hardware solutions that allow users to disable cameras and microphones.

Notable insights include the idea that Meta's push into AR/VR is a strategic move to establish a foothold in what they see as the next major tech platform, as well as the recognition that users have been unknowingly contributing to machine learning training data through various online interactions for years.
