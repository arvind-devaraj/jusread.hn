Ask HN: Do you also "hoard" notes/links but struggle to turn them into actions?  (235 pts, 217 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46826277

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a user exploring the concept of "Concerns," aimed at addressing the common issue of capturing notes and links without effectively turning them into actionable tasks. The user, a builder of the note-taking app Obsidian, seeks feedback on the challenges people face with their "second brain" systems, particularly in the areas of capture, organization, retrieval, and execution. They propose an "action engine" that would help surface relevant saved material and suggest concrete next actions based on active projects.

In the comments, several recurring themes emerge. Many users express frustration with the retrieval process, emphasizing the need for better search capabilities and on-demand recall. Privacy concerns are also significant, with many preferring local-first solutions that avoid cloud storage. Some commenters suggest that the real issue may be less about organization and more about reducing the intake of information, advocating for filtered digests instead of hoarding notes.

Notable insights include the idea that a more structured approach to note-taking, such as using block-based systems like Logseq, can enhance retrieval and reduce the cognitive load of organizing information. Additionally, there is a consensus that any tool suggesting actions should provide clear confidence signals and avoid overwhelming users with noise or irrelevant suggestions. Overall, the discussion highlights a shared struggle with managing information overload and the desire for tools that facilitate actionable outcomes from captured knowledge.
