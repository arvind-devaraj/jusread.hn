Don't just paste the AI at me  (181 pts, 113 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48242648

The Hacker News discussion titled "Don't just paste the AI at me" revolves around the frustrations of individuals who feel that simply pasting AI-generated responses in conversations is unprofessional and dismissive. The original post criticizes the practice of using AI to answer questions that could be easily researched, arguing that it undermines meaningful human interaction and communication.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and debate. Many commenters express that while they understand the annoyance of receiving AI responses, the way to address this issue should be more tactful and considerate. Some argue that pasting AI responses is rude and reflects a lack of effort, while others believe that asking questions that could be answered by AI is also problematic. Notably, some users highlight the importance of human connection and the value of engaging in conversations rather than relying solely on AI for information.

Overall, the discussion reveals a tension between the convenience of AI and the need for genuine human interaction, with various opinions on how to navigate this evolving landscape.
