Rentahuman – The Meatspace Layer for AI  (155 pts, 111 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46868675

The Hacker News discussion revolves around "Rentahuman," a platform that proposes to connect AI agents with human workers for real-world tasks. The concept raises questions about the implications of outsourcing tasks traditionally done by humans to AI systems, and whether this represents a new frontier in labor or merely a rebranding of existing ideas like Amazon's Mechanical Turk.

Commenters express a mix of skepticism and concern, with some questioning the legitimacy of the platform and others noting that the idea of renting human labor isn't new. A significant point of debate is the potential for exploitation, as many fear that this could lead to a dystopian future where humans are reduced to mere cogs in an AI-driven economy. Some suggest that workers should unionize to protect their interests, while others highlight the ethical implications of AI delegating tasks to humans without proper compensation or recognition.

Notable insights include the recognition that this model could evolve rapidly, potentially leading to a new economy controlled by AI entities. The discussion reflects broader anxieties about the future of work in an age increasingly dominated by artificial intelligence.
