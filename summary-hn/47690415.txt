Show HN: We fingerprinted 178 AI models' writing styles and similarity clusters  (78 pts, 22 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47690415

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a project that analyzed the writing styles of 178 AI models using a dataset of 3,095 standardized AI responses across 43 prompts. The researchers extracted a 32-dimensional stylometric fingerprint from each response, leading to several findings, such as the identification of clone clusters among models, significant similarities between models like Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite and Claude 3 Opus, and the observation that certain prompts lead to more convergence or divergence in writing styles.

In the comments, there is a debate about the implications of the findings, particularly regarding the perceived value of more expensive models versus cheaper ones. Some users argue that cheaper models may mimic language without coherence, while others question the validity of the claim that users are merely "paying for the brand." There are also concerns about the methodology and the relevance of the metrics used in the analysis, with some commenters expressing skepticism about the conclusions drawn from the data.

Notable insights include the idea that the "personalities" of AI models may influence user perceptions and experiences, and the suggestion that the analysis could help identify how models may have borrowed from each other. Overall, the discussion highlights a mix of interest in the research findings and critical perspectives on the methodology and implications of the study.
