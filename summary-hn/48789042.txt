$85,000 in tokens later: What I learned from scaling agentic coding at Lovable  (21 pts, 32 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48789042

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a blog post detailing the author's experience scaling "agentic coding" at Lovable, a platform that utilizes AI to assist in coding. The author claims to have spent $85,000 on tokens for AI-generated code, highlighting a significant increase in productivity, with a reported jump from 20-30 merged pull requests (PRs) per week to 150. However, the post has been criticized for lacking substantive insights and for promoting a potentially misleading narrative about the effectiveness of AI in coding.

In the comments, there is a robust debate about the implications of relying on AI for coding, particularly in critical sectors like banking and infrastructure. Some commenters argue that while AI can enhance productivity for non-critical applications, it poses risks in more sensitive areas. Others express skepticism about the actual value derived from the author's investment, questioning whether the productivity gains are meaningful or if they merely reflect a superficial increase in activity without real oversight or quality assurance.

Notable opinions include concerns about the long-term effects of over-reliance on AI tools, with some commenters warning that it could erode essential coding skills among engineers. Additionally, there is criticism of the marketing tactics used by platforms like Lovable, which some feel mislead users into believing they can easily create successful applications without adequate support or understanding of the underlying technology. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of enthusiasm for AI's potential and caution regarding its practical application in software development.
