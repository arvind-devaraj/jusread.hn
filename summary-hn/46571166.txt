Show HN: GlyphLang – An AI-first programming language  (44 pts, 27 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46571166

The Hacker News discussion centers around GlyphLang, a newly developed programming language designed to be generated by AI rather than written by humans. The creator built GlyphLang to address token limit issues encountered while using AI models like Claude, which struggle with context accumulation in lengthy coding sessions. GlyphLang uses concise symbols to replace verbose keywords, resulting in a more efficient tokenization process—reportedly reducing token usage by about 45% compared to Python and 63% compared to Java.

In the comments, users debate the effectiveness of GlyphLang's approach, with some expressing skepticism about the practicality of using short symbols, arguing that they may lead to confusion or collisions in token recognition by AI models. Others highlight the importance of maintaining human readability and familiarity in programming languages, suggesting that overly terse syntax could hinder both AI generation and human understanding. Notably, some commenters propose that instead of creating new languages, efforts should focus on improving existing ones to enhance their compatibility with AI models.

Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of enthusiasm for GlyphLang's innovative concept and caution regarding its implementation, emphasizing the balance between AI optimization and human usability in programming languages.
