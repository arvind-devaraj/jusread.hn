Show HN: ContextVault – Shared memory layer for your AI and your team  (11 pts, 10 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48900288

The Hacker News discussion centers around ContextVault, a tool developed by Kevin to address the fragmentation of context and information when using AI tools across projects. ContextVault allows teams to store and organize reusable context, such as prompts and coding conventions, in a shared memory layer that can be accessed by various AI clients. The tool aims to streamline workflows by reducing duplicated efforts and making previously shared knowledge easily retrievable.

In the comments, users debate the specifics of the tool's functionality, particularly the limits on queries and memory storage, with some seeking clarification on how context is stored and retrieved. There are discussions about the nature of the stored context, with some users comparing it to Markdown files and others emphasizing its structured, vectorized format. Concerns about data security and privacy are also raised, with users questioning how sensitive information is managed and deleted.

Notable insights include the recognition of the tool's potential to enhance collaboration within teams, especially for non-developers who may struggle with AI context management. Users express interest in how ContextVault differentiates itself from traditional documentation methods and highlight the importance of maintaining shared knowledge even when team members leave. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of curiosity and caution regarding the implications of using such a tool in professional settings.
