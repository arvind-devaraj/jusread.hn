What is happening to writing? Cognitive debt, Claude Code, the space around AI  (135 pts, 116 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47061642

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the evolving nature of writing in the context of AI's rise, particularly focusing on the distinction between "writing" and "content." The original post suggests that traditional human writing may be overshadowed by AI-generated content, which raises concerns about the quality and originality of what consumers will value. The term "cognitive debt" is introduced, hinting at the mental toll of sifting through an overwhelming amount of low-quality content.

In the comments, there is a consensus that the quality of writing is diminishing, with some likening AI-generated content to fast food compared to fine dining. Participants debate the implications of AI on the writing landscape, with some arguing that AI can produce low-quality content at scale, while others believe it cannot replicate the depth and nuance of high-quality human writing. Notably, discussions touch on the historical context of "post-truth" and the influence of mass media on public perception, suggesting that the challenges of discerning quality in writing are not new but have been exacerbated by technological advancements.

Several commenters express skepticism about the future of writing, with some lamenting the decline of meaningful content and others questioning whether AI can ever truly replace the unique insights and experiences that human authors bring to their work. The conversation reflects a broader anxiety about the impact of AI on creativity and the value of genuine human expression in an increasingly automated world.
