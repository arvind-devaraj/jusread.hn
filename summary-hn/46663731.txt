Data Activation Thoughts  (21 pts, 5 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46663731

The Hacker News discussion titled "Data Activation Thoughts" revolves around the evolving concept of "data moats" in the context of large language models (LLMs) and their ability to utilize various data types, particularly in healthcare and biobank settings. The original poster reflects on how the value of having data is shifting towards making it useful for LLMs, citing recent efforts to convert structured medical data into reasoning traces that enhance LLM performance, though these methods are still in development.

In the comments, there is a debate about the best approaches to training models with specific datasets, with one user emphasizing that decisions on fine-tuning or reasoning training depend on factors like cost and frequency of use. Another commenter shares insights from the legal tech sector, noting that many law firms find existing AI tools underwhelming despite having contracts for them, highlighting a disconnect between technology offerings and practical utility.

Notable insights include the recognition that while data moats may be eroding, the challenge lies in effectively leveraging data for LLMs. Additionally, the legal tech discussion underscores a broader concern about the adoption and effectiveness of AI tools across industries, suggesting that simply having technology is not enough if it does not meet user needs.
