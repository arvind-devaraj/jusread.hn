Labor market impacts of AI: A new measure and early evidence  (333 pts, 547 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47268391

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a research post from Anthropic that examines the labor market impacts of AI, particularly focusing on how AI adoption may be affecting hiring rates, especially for younger workers. The research suggests a notable slowdown in job-finding rates for young workers in occupations exposed to AI, prompting discussions about the broader implications of AI on employment.

In the comments, there is a mix of skepticism and concern regarding the actual impact of AI on productivity and hiring practices. Some commenters argue that while AI may enhance productivity for those who know how to use it, it hasn't significantly changed the overall job market dynamics. Others suggest that companies might be using AI as an excuse for hiring freezes or layoffs, rather than a genuine reflection of productivity improvements. Notably, there are critiques of management practices that foster insecurity among employees, as well as discussions about the disconnect between increased speed in software development and the quality of outputs.

Several commenters express frustration with management's expectations for AI to deliver substantial productivity boosts, while others highlight the ongoing challenges in resource allocation and decision-making within organizations. Overall, the conversation reflects a blend of optimism and skepticism about AI's role in the labor market, with a strong emphasis on the need for clarity and effective leadership in navigating these changes.
