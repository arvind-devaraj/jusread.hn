Ask HN: Do you read differently now that anything could be AI generated?  (35 pts, 31 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47902507

The Hacker News discussion titled "Ask HN: Do you read differently now that anything could be AI generated?" explores how the rise of AI-generated content has impacted reading habits and perceptions of written material. Users share their experiences and concerns about distinguishing between human and AI authorship, particularly in books, articles, and online content.

A significant point of debate is the skepticism towards AI-generated works. Many commenters express a strong preference for human-created content, with some stating they avoid reading anything published after 2022 unless highly recommended. Others emphasize the importance of curation and trust in sources, noting that AI-generated content often feels generic and lacks the depth of human creativity. Notable insights include the idea that specificity and unique perspectives are now more valuable, as generic correctness is perceived as less meaningful. Additionally, some users highlight a shift towards physical books and a more critical approach to online content, reflecting a broader concern about the quality and authenticity of information in an AI-saturated landscape.
