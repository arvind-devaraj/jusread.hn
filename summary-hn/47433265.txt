Autoresearch for SAT Solvers  (167 pts, 25 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47433265

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a GitHub project titled "Autoresearch for SAT Solvers," which explores the use of autonomous agents to improve SAT (Boolean satisfiability) solvers. The project raises questions about the originality of the techniques discovered by the agent, particularly in light of the fact that many competing solvers, including Z3, were not included in recent competitions like MaxSAT 2024. Commenters debate whether the agent's improvements are genuinely novel or simply derived from existing solvers and their training data.

Key points of agreement include the recognition that the agent may have benefited from techniques in its training data, leading to concerns about overfitting and the potential lack of algorithmic innovation in the project's commit history. Some commenters express skepticism about the agent's ability to generalize its findings to real-world applications beyond benchmark instances, emphasizing the complexity of SAT problems in practical scenarios. Notable insights include the comparison to traditional parameter tuning methods and the acknowledgment that while parameter tuning has been a longstanding practice, the complexity of NP-hard problems may necessitate more sophisticated approaches. Overall, the discussion highlights both the potential and limitations of using autonomous agents in the field of SAT solving.
