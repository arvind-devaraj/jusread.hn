Why I may ‘hire’ AI instead of a graduate student  (117 pts, 110 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47396557

The Hacker News discussion revolves around an article that explores the potential of hiring AI for research tasks instead of graduate students. The author raises concerns about the changing dynamics in academia, particularly the implications of relying on AI for research and the diminishing role of graduate students in the process.

In the comments, there is a debate about the necessity of hiring junior employees versus the efficiency of AI. Some commenters argue that forced hiring initiatives for junior positions could help address the skills gap in the workforce, while others express skepticism about the effectiveness of such measures. There is also a discussion about the role of universities in training students and the importance of mentorship in developing future researchers.

Notable insights include the recognition that while AI can enhance research productivity, it cannot replace the educational and developmental aspects of graduate training. Commenters highlight the need for a balance between research output and teaching responsibilities, with some advocating for a decoupling of these roles in academia. Overall, the conversation reflects a broader concern about the future of education and the workforce in an increasingly automated world.
