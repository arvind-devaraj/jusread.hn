What happens when you post a real Monet and say it's AI?  (85 pts, 74 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48134400

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a Twitter post that questions the perception of art when a real Monet painting is presented as AI-generated. The central theme is the reaction of people who, upon being told the artwork was AI-created, expressed negative opinions, highlighting biases against AI art.

In the comments, there is a debate about the nature of originality and artistic value. Some users argue that AI can imitate styles like Monet's but cannot create original art, while others contend that human artists also draw from existing influences. A significant point of contention is whether the context of creation (human vs. AI) affects the appreciation of art. Many commenters suggest that people's judgments about art are often shallow and influenced by preconceived notions rather than the artwork itself.

Notable insights include the idea that the emotional and intentional aspects of human-created art are crucial to its value, and that biases against AI-generated art may reveal deeper issues about how society perceives creativity and authenticity. The discussion also touches on the complexities of art appreciation and the challenges of changing entrenched opinions.
