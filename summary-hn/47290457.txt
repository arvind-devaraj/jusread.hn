Training students to prove they're not robots is pushing them to use more AI  (162 pts, 173 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47290457

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the challenges of training students to demonstrate their originality in writing, particularly in the context of increasing reliance on AI tools. The article highlights concerns that educational systems are pushing students to conform to formulaic writing styles to prove they are not using AI, which may ultimately stifle creativity and genuine expression.

In the comments, there is a consensus that traditional grading methods often favor formulaic writing, which can demotivate students and hinder their development as authentic writers. Some commenters argue that while formulaic writing is a skill, it should not overshadow the importance of creativity and effective communication. There is also a debate about the effectiveness of AI detection tools, with some expressing skepticism about their reliability and the implications of punishing students based on their outputs.

Notable insights include the idea that students may intentionally write in a less sophisticated manner to avoid being accused of using AI, reflecting a broader concern about the pressures of academic integrity and the authenticity of communication. Additionally, some participants suggest that fostering a love for reading and diverse writing styles may be more beneficial for students than rigid adherence to formulaic structures.
