Tell HN: don't trust Bigco AI agents with AI research IP  (19 pts, 7 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48798385

The Hacker News discussion revolves around concerns about sharing AI research intellectual property (IP) with large AI companies, such as OpenAI and Anthropic. The original poster expresses paranoia that these companies, driven by competitive motives, may exploit shared data to undermine competitors. They argue that even if companies claim they won't misuse this information, there are subtle ways they could benefit from it, such as training their models on it or using human inspectors to draw inspiration from it.

In the comments, there is a consensus that trusting big AI companies with any form of IP is risky. Many commenters agree that these companies have shown intentions to protect their interests and may resort to unethical practices to maintain a competitive edge. Some highlight that the issue extends beyond AI research, suggesting that these companies are likely to appropriate ideas and data from various domains. Notably, one commenter points out that terms of service often allow companies to use user-generated content for training, reinforcing the idea that users should be cautious about what they share. Overall, the discussion underscores a widespread skepticism about the ethical practices of large AI firms regarding IP.
