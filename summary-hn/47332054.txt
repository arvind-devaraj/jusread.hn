TADA: Speech generation through text-acoustic synchronization  (103 pts, 27 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47332054

The Hacker News discussion revolves around TADA, a new speech generation model that synchronizes text and acoustic representations to produce more natural-sounding speech. The model aligns audio representations directly with text tokens, creating a continuous stream where both elements move in sync, which is a departure from traditional methods that compress audio into fixed-rate frames.

In the comments, there is a debate about the feasibility of running TADA on CPUs versus GPUs. Some users argue that while CPUs are cheaper and more accessible, they are generally less efficient for tasks like matrix multiplication compared to GPUs. Others highlight the importance of using available resources, especially in scenarios where GPU power is already allocated to other tasks. Additionally, there are discussions about the quality of the generated speech, with some users noting peculiarities in the audio output, such as a "vocal fry" effect and subtle modulation issues.

Notable insights include the recognition that TADA's one-to-one alignment of text and audio tokens could lead to mid-sentence pauses if not continuously fed with text, and that while it can generate speech based on prompts, it may not perform as well as more advanced models in certain applications. The model is open-source under the MIT license and supports multiple languages, which adds to its accessibility for developers.
