OpenAI hit with lawsuit claiming ChatGPT acted as an unlicensed lawyer  (16 pts, 6 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47299426

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a lawsuit against OpenAI, alleging that ChatGPT acted as an unlicensed lawyer by providing legal advice that led to poor submissions in a legal case. The plaintiff is represented by Sidley Austin, a major law firm, raising concerns about the legal implications of AI-generated advice.

Commenters express a mix of skepticism and concern regarding the lawsuit's validity. Some argue that it seems unreasonable to hold OpenAI accountable for the misuse of its technology by an individual, likening it to blaming a car manufacturer for accidents caused by drivers. Others highlight the financial motivations of large law firms to take on such cases, regardless of their likelihood of success. There is also a call for accountability in providing legal and medical advice, emphasizing the importance of professional oversight in areas with significant consequences.

Overall, the discussion reflects a broader debate about the responsibilities of tech companies in the context of AI and the potential risks of unregulated advice in critical fields.
