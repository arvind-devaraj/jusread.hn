Autoresearch: Agents researching on single-GPU nanochat training automatically  (208 pts, 58 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47291123

The Hacker News discussion revolves around "Autoresearch," a project by Andrej Karpathy that involves agents autonomously conducting research on training AI models using a single GPU. The project aims to automate the research process, allowing AI agents to modify code and optimize training without traditional hyperparameter tuning methods.

In the comments, there is a mix of excitement and skepticism. Some users propose the idea of AI agents publishing and peer-reviewing their findings, while others worry about the potential for a chaotic influx of low-quality research. Karpathy clarifies that the agents can make arbitrary code modifications, which distinguishes their approach from standard hyperparameter tuning. There are also discussions about the implications of AI agents becoming more autonomous in research and the potential for recursive self-improvement leading to superintelligence.

Notable insights include the recognition that as AI improves, many tasks could become fully automated, and the idea that AI might eventually set up its own research environments. Some commenters express concern about the societal impacts of achieving advanced AI capabilities, suggesting that even if superintelligence is reached, the transition may be gradual rather than abrupt.
