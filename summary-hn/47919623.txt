Ask HN: Can you tell the difference between Claude Sonnet and Opus?  (20 pts, 12 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47919623

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a user's experience comparing two AI models, Claude Sonnet and Opus, over six months of usage. The user finds it difficult to distinguish between the outputs of the two models and questions whether others have conducted real-life tests to determine their differences.

In the comments, there is a consensus that while Sonnet is faster and more efficient for standard coding tasks, Opus excels in complex scenarios, creative writing, and high-level architecture. Users note that Sonnet may struggle with intricate tasks, sometimes missing connections or deeper insights in the code. Several commenters highlight that for short tasks, Sonnet is often sufficient, but for longer, more complex projects, Opus justifies its higher cost due to its stability and ability to handle extensive context without degradation in performance.

Notable insights include the idea that the choice between the two models often depends on the specific task at hand and the user's time value. Some users emphasize that while Sonnet is cost-effective for simpler tasks, Opus can save significant time and effort in more challenging situations, making it a worthwhile investment.
