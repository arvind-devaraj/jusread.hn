Show HN: I speak 5 languages. Common apps taught me none. So I built lairner  (35 pts, 86 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47003596

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a language-learning platform called lairner, created by Tim, who speaks five languages. Frustrated by traditional language apps that focus on gamified exercises rather than practical speaking skills, Tim developed lairner to offer over 700 courses in 70+ languages, including lesser-known and endangered languages. He emphasizes that lairner allows users to learn from languages they already know, rather than just English, and aims to provide a more effective learning experience.

In the comments, users express interest in the platform and inquire about the addition of specific languages, such as Maltese. There is a debate about the app's effectiveness compared to established platforms like Duolingo, with some users questioning its teaching methods and accuracy. Notable insights include discussions on the definition of fluency and comprehension, with varying opinions on what constitutes effective language learning. Some commenters express skepticism about the app's quality, suggesting that it may not meet the standards of established language-learning tools. Overall, the conversation highlights both enthusiasm for lairner's unique approach and concerns about its execution.
