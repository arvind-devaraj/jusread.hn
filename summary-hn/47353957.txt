Show HN: Understudy – Teach a desktop agent by demonstrating a task once  (120 pts, 21 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47353957

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a project called Understudy, a desktop agent designed to automate tasks across various applications and platforms by learning through demonstration. The creator explains that users can teach the agent a task once, and it will record the necessary actions and intents to replicate the task in the future. Currently, Understudy is only available for macOS, with plans for future development on other platforms.

In the comments, there is a mix of appreciation for the innovative concept and criticism regarding its macOS exclusivity, with some users arguing that Linux and Windows are underserved markets. A debate arises about the robustness of the agent's learning capabilities, with some commenters expressing skepticism about its ability to handle edge cases effectively. Others highlight the importance of iterative learning, suggesting that mastering tasks typically requires multiple attempts.

Notable insights include the acknowledgment that while the demo showcased a successful task completion, real-world applications may involve more complexity. The creator also emphasizes the potential for the project to expand beyond macOS, inviting collaboration and further development from the community.
