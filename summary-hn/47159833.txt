Steering interpretable language models with concept algebra  (77 pts, 8 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47159833

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a post introducing "concept algebra," a method for steering language models by injecting, suppressing, and composing human-understandable concepts during inference without the need for retraining or prompt engineering. The author invites feedback on potential benchmarking tasks, failure cases, and the practical utility of this compositional control.

In the comments, users express interest in quantifying the effectiveness of this steering method compared to traditional prompt engineering, particularly regarding the suppression of unwanted outputs. One commenter is eager for a concept dictionary to explore moral scenarios using the framework, while another inquires about the method's applicability in maintaining focus for function-calling agents. The author acknowledges these requests and indicates that further technical details and resources will be shared soon.

Overall, the discussion highlights a mix of curiosity and skepticism about the practical applications and effectiveness of concept algebra in language models, with a strong emphasis on the need for empirical validation and resources to facilitate its use.
