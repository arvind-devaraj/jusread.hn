Peppa Pig studio wants to clone child actors' voices with AI indefinitely  (23 pts, 16 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48701902

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the Peppa Pig studio's intention to use AI to clone the voices of child actors indefinitely. This raises concerns about the implications for the actors and the nature of content creation. Many commenters express their disapproval of the show's portrayal of stereotypes, particularly regarding gender roles and the depiction of parental figures, with some suggesting that the show conveys negative messages to children.

The debate highlights a divide between those who find the show's content problematic and those who are more accepting of its humor. Some commenters argue that the use of AI to replicate voices is unsettling and detracts from the natural evolution of storytelling, emphasizing the importance of new talent and the organic growth of characters. Others note that since child actors age out of their roles, AI might not necessarily take away job opportunities but could instead create a strange dynamic in content creation. Overall, the discussion reflects broader concerns about the ethical implications of AI in entertainment and the cultural messages conveyed to young audiences.
