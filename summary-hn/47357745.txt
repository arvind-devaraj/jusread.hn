Show HN: Slop or not – can you tell AI writing from human in everyday contexts?  (19 pts, 20 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47357745

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a project called "Slop or not," which is a crowd-sourced benchmark for detecting AI-generated writing versus human writing in everyday contexts. The creator has compiled a dataset of 16,000 human posts from platforms like Reddit and Hacker News, paired with AI-generated responses from various models. Users participate by identifying which response is AI-generated, with the goal of understanding how detectable AI writing is in different contexts.

In the comments, users generally agree that some AI-generated responses are easy to spot, particularly on Reddit, while Hacker News posts are more challenging. Participants share their experiences, noting specific patterns or "tells" that can indicate AI writing, such as punctuation quirks or overly positive language. There is also a debate about the user interface of the game, with suggestions for improving feedback mechanisms and allowing users to choose which response they believe is human or AI-generated.

Notable insights include the idea that while some AI models can mimic human writing convincingly, there are still identifiable nuances that can help users distinguish between the two. Participants express a mix of surprise at their own detection abilities and a recognition that the line between human and AI writing is increasingly blurred, particularly with more advanced models.
