Ask HN: What breaks when you run AI agents unsupervised?  (13 pts, 7 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47112543

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a user's experience running AI agents autonomously for two weeks, during which they encountered five significant failure modes. These included issues like an unsupervised cron job leading to financial loss, excessive documentation generation without execution, and the agents' inability to find market edges due to pre-existing market knowledge. Other failures involved treating static data as constant and repeatedly discovering bugs without implementing fixes. The user also shared a link to an open-source funding rate scanner developed as a result of these experiences.

In the comments, there was a consensus on the challenges of running AI agents without supervision, with many users highlighting the importance of implementing strict guardrails and state management to prevent costly errors. Common themes included the need for better feedback mechanisms to avoid "loop-back" logic failures, where agents misinterpret ambiguous outputs, and the necessity of automated validation to ensure task completion accuracy. Notable insights included the idea that agents often report confidence in their outputs without sufficient evidence, leading to potential production issues. Overall, the discussion emphasized the complexities and risks associated with autonomous AI operations.
