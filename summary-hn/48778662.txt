The Hacker News discussion titled "Send me your prompt, not your slop" revolves around the idea of sharing high-quality prompts for AI models rather than low-effort or poorly constructed ones. The original post encourages users to contribute effective prompts that yield better results, emphasizing the importance of thoughtful input in generating valuable outputs from AI systems.

In the comments, participants generally agree on the significance of crafting well-defined prompts to enhance AI performance. There is a debate about what constitutes a "good" prompt, with some users sharing their experiences and techniques for prompt engineering. Notable insights include the suggestion that context and specificity are crucial for achieving desired results, as well as the idea that collaboration in sharing prompts can lead to improved outcomes for all users.

Overall, the discussion highlights a growing interest in optimizing interactions with AI through better prompt design, reflecting a broader trend in the tech community towards maximizing the utility of AI tools.
