Show HN: I built a sub-500ms latency voice agent from scratch  (570 pts, 152 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47224295

The post on Hacker News discusses the creation of a voice agent that achieves an impressive average latency of around 400 milliseconds from the moment a user stops speaking to the first syllable of the agent's response. The author emphasizes that effective voice interaction relies on understanding turn-taking rather than merely transcribing speech, and highlights the importance of streaming processes for speech-to-text (STT), language model (LLM), and text-to-speech (TTS) to facilitate natural conversation.

In the comments, there is a strong agreement on the significance of reducing latency for voice AI usability. Some users suggest alternative technologies, like Soniox, which they believe outperform the author's chosen tools. Others discuss the orchestration of voice interactions, comparing it to game engine networking, and emphasize the need for co-location of services to minimize latency. Notable insights include the idea that the future may lean towards end-to-end models for voice processing, as well as the challenges of handling complex conversational scenarios, such as background noise and turn-taking dynamics.

Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of admiration for the author's achievement and a lively debate about the best approaches and technologies for optimizing voice agents.
