AI is not a coworker, it's an exoskeleton  (522 pts, 527 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47078324

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the concept of AI as an "exoskeleton" rather than a coworker, suggesting that AI tools should enhance human capabilities rather than replace them. The original post argues that AI should be viewed as a supportive technology that augments human work, rather than an independent agent operating in the economy.

In the comments, there is significant debate about the implications of AI functioning as an independent agent. Some users express concern about the potential dangers of such AI, suggesting that if it were to operate without human oversight, it should be eliminated. Others discuss the current limitations of AI, particularly in complex tasks like chess, where traditional algorithms outperform large language models (LLMs). There is also a consensus that the effectiveness of AI largely depends on the quality and quantity of data available for training.

Notable insights include the recognition that while LLMs can perform certain tasks, they often lack true understanding and reasoning capabilities, which are crucial for tasks like chess. Some commenters argue that LLMs are not the ideal approach for games that require strategic foresight, emphasizing the need for more specialized AI techniques. Overall, the discussion highlights both the potential and the limitations of AI in augmenting human work and the ongoing debate about its role in the future of labor.
