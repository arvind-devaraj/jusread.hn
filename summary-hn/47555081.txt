The risk of AI isn't making us lazy, but making "lazy" look productive  (76 pts, 88 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47555081

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the concern that AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), may encourage superficial learning among engineers by making it easier to access concise summaries of complex topics. The original post argues that while AI can enhance research efficiency, it risks making "lazy" learning appear productive, ultimately weakening critical thinking and deep understanding. The author advocates for using AI as a tool for deeper cognitive engagement rather than as a shortcut for shallow learning.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and debate. Some users emphasize the importance of maintaining critical thinking and domain expertise to effectively leverage AI, while others highlight the potential for AI to enhance understanding by clarifying complex material. A few commenters express concern about the implications of relying too heavily on AI, suggesting that it could lead to a decline in genuine productivity and learning. Notably, some participants share personal experiences of using AI to facilitate deeper engagement with material, illustrating the potential for AI to serve as a cognitive amplifier when used thoughtfully.

Overall, the discussion underscores the need for a balanced approach to AI, where it is utilized to enhance deep work rather than replace the rigorous processes of learning and critical thinking.
