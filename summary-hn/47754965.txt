Why most AI projects feel useless  (10 pts, 9 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47754965

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the sentiment that many AI projects currently feel redundant or unoriginal, as they often appear to be variations of existing tools rather than innovative solutions. The original poster expresses frustration with the proliferation of similar AI applications, suggesting that they lack unique value.

In the comments, several users agree that the market is saturated with AI tools that often mimic one another without addressing specific needs. Some point out that many projects are driven by hype rather than genuine problem-solving, with a focus on quick launches rather than quality. Others highlight the importance of finding niche applications that require engineering skills to differentiate from the crowd. A recurring theme is the lowering barrier to entry for AI development, which has led to a flood of mediocre products.

Notable insights include the idea that successful AI tools need to integrate deeply into workflows and capture valuable data, rather than just relying on AI models. Additionally, some commenters reflect on the synthetic nature of many AI-generated solutions, suggesting a lack of authentic creativity in the current landscape. Overall, the discussion underscores a growing concern about the quality and originality of AI projects in a rapidly evolving market.
