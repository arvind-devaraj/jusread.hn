The skill of the future is not 'AI', but 'Focus' (2025)  (68 pts, 15 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46513728

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the idea that the essential skill for the future will be "focus" rather than just technical proficiency in AI. The original post suggests that as AI takes over routine tasks, humans will need to engage in deeper, more meaningful work, emphasizing the importance of concentration and critical thinking.

In the comments, there is a consensus that while AI can enhance productivity, it may also lead to a decline in problem-solving skills if over-relied upon. Some users highlight the distinction between problem-solving and creative thinking, arguing that AI should be used as a tool to augment human capabilities rather than replace them. There are also reflections on workplace culture, with some commenters expressing frustration over the prevalence of back-to-back meetings that hinder deep work.

Notable insights include the notion that ethical behavior will also be a crucial skill in the future, alongside focus. Additionally, some commenters express a desire for a shift in workplace dynamics, where being perpetually busy is no longer seen as a status symbol, and where the value of deep, focused work is recognized and prioritized.
