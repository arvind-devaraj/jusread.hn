PA bench: Evaluating web agents on real world personal assistant workflows  (38 pts, 9 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47157160

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a blog post by Vibrant Labs introducing PA Bench, a benchmark designed to evaluate web agents' performance in handling multi-step workflows using simulated Gmail and Calendar environments. The team noted that existing benchmarks failed to address key failure modes encountered in real-world applications, particularly as complexity increased. They are currently expanding their dataset and simulations to include more complex enterprise workflows and are seeking feedback on their benchmark results.

In the comments, users debated the practicality of using web agents for mundane tasks like checking emails and managing calendars. Some argued that it might be more efficient to use specialized tools rather than relying on browser agents, citing concerns over token costs and safety. Others highlighted the potential for integrating multiple agents from different providers to optimize task execution and avoid common failures, such as permission issues. Notably, there was a recognition of the unique training methods employed by Vibrant Labs, suggesting that their approach could lead to faster advancements in the field. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of skepticism and curiosity about the future of web agents in personal assistant roles.
