Show HN: Ktx – Open-source executable context layer for data agents  (93 pts, 37 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48309986

The Hacker News discussion centers around the open-source project "ktx," which is an executable context layer designed to enhance the reliability of data agents in analytics environments. The creators of ktx developed it to address common issues faced when building production-grade data agents, particularly around SQL accuracy. They highlight problems like outdated columns, incorrect joins, and attribution logic failures that can lead to misleading analytics results. Ktx aims to provide a structured way to manage business context and SQL generation through a combination of Markdown wiki pages and YAML files that define data structures and relationships.

In the comments, users engage in discussions comparing ktx to similar tools like Wren and OpenVikings, with ktx being positioned as a hybrid solution that combines a semantic layer with a knowledge base. Questions arise about accuracy measurement, the evolution of business context, and the potential for sandbox environments for testing queries. Notable insights include the importance of context management for data agents and the challenges of maintaining accurate and up-to-date information in dynamic data environments. Overall, the community expresses interest in the project and its potential to improve data agent reliability.
