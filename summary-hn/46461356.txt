How to use AI to augment learning without losing critical thinking skills?  (24 pts, 14 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46461356

The Hacker News discussion centers around the use of AI as a learning tool while maintaining critical thinking skills. The original poster shares their experience of using AI for tasks like generating code, simplifying complex concepts, and fact-checking, but expresses concern about becoming overly reliant on it. They seek advice on how to enhance their AI usage without compromising their cognitive abilities.

In the comments, there is a consensus that using AI blindly can lead to a decline in critical thinking skills. Many users suggest strategies to mitigate this risk, such as introducing "friction" by delaying AI responses, using AI for review rather than initial problem-solving, and ensuring a deep understanding of AI-generated solutions. Some commenters emphasize the importance of balancing AI assistance with independent learning and problem-solving to foster critical thinking.

Notable insights include the idea that incorrect AI outputs can prompt users to question and verify information, potentially reinforcing critical thinking. Others highlight the need for a structured approach to using AI, such as treating it as a brainstorming partner or a tutor, rather than a crutch. Overall, the discussion reflects a nuanced view of AI's role in education, advocating for mindful and strategic usage.
