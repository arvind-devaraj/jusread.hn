The post titled "AI should elevate your thinking, not replace it" discusses the role of artificial intelligence in enhancing human cognitive abilities rather than substituting them. The author argues that while AI can assist in various tasks, it should not diminish the necessity for critical thinking and problem-solving skills, particularly in fields like engineering and software development.

In the comments, there is a lively debate about the quality of education in engineering and computer science programs. Some commenters argue that many graduates lack critical thinking skills, suggesting that degrees can be obtained through rote memorization rather than genuine understanding. Others counter that a degree still requires a level of critical thinking, even if it varies by institution. There is also a discussion about the relevance of formal education versus practical experience, with some asserting that what matters most is what individuals can produce rather than their academic credentials.

Notable insights include concerns about the increasing reliance on AI for tasks that require human judgment and creativity, with some commenters fearing that this could lead to a decline in essential skills. Others express skepticism about the effectiveness of current educational programs in preparing students for real-world challenges, emphasizing the need for a balance between leveraging AI and maintaining critical thinking capabilities.
