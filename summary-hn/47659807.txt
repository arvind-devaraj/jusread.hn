Ask HN: How do systems (or people) detect when a text is written by an LLM  (44 pts, 62 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47659807

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the question of how systems or individuals can detect whether a text was generated by a large language model (LLM). The original poster is curious about existing APIs or methods for this detection.

In the comments, there is a consensus that humans often rely on pattern recognition to identify LLM-generated text, while automated systems may use machine learning models trained on AI-generated content. Pangram is mentioned as a notable detector with a low rate of false positives, and it has an API available for use. However, some commenters argue that detection is not foolproof, as many people have developed biases about certain writing styles that they associate with LLMs, leading to unnecessary adjustments in their own writing.

Notable insights include the idea that specific stylistic elements, such as the use of em dashes, overly simplistic lists, and certain repetitive phrases, can signal LLM-generated text. Some commenters also highlight the monotony and lack of depth in LLM writing as key indicators. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of skepticism about the reliability of detection methods and the evolving understanding of LLM writing characteristics.
