Ask HN: Has anyone replaced Claude/GPT with a local model for daily coding?  (1318 pts, 563 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48542100

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the question of whether users have successfully replaced cloud-based AI models like Claude or GPT with local models for daily coding tasks. The original poster seeks insights into setups and performance metrics from those who have made the switch.

In the comments, several users share their experiences with local models, noting that while some have achieved decent performance, many find that local setups often lag behind cloud offerings in terms of speed (tokens per second). Users discuss the challenges of hardware costs, electricity consumption, and the lack of enterprise tools for selecting and running local models. There is a consensus that while local models can provide privacy and independence from cloud services, they often struggle with performance and usability compared to their cloud counterparts.

Notable insights include discussions about the specific hardware configurations that yield better performance, the potential for reinforcement learning to fine-tune models, and the varying experiences with different models like Qwen and Gemma. Some users express skepticism about the practicality of local models for serious coding tasks, while others highlight the benefits of having a self-hosted solution for privacy and control. Overall, the conversation reflects a mix of optimism and caution regarding the viability of local AI models for everyday coding.
