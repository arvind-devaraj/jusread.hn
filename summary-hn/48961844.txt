The Hacker News discussion titled "Ask HN: Do you have any plan for post AGI?" revolves around the potential consequences of artificial general intelligence (AGI) on employment and society. The original post prompts readers to consider their plans if AI were to replace their jobs, sparking a variety of responses.

In the comments, there is a mix of skepticism and concern about the implications of AGI. Some users suggest drastic actions like a general strike or joining rebels, while others question the feasibility of such actions if jobs disappear entirely. A recurring theme is the idea that a post-AGI economy may not rely on human demand, leading to debates about the future of capitalism and the necessity of jobs. Some commenters express a belief that AGI will not be achieved through current AI technologies, while others argue that advancements in AI are inevitable and will continue to evolve.

Notable insights include a pessimistic view on the potential societal impacts of AGI, with some users referencing historical critiques of industrialization and capitalism. Others express a desire to escape or adapt to a changing world, with plans ranging from traveling to living a simpler life. Overall, the discussion highlights a mix of anxiety, hope, and philosophical contemplation regarding the future of work and society in the face of advancing AI technologies.
