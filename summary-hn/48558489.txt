Has AI already killed self-help nonfiction books?  (422 pts, 489 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48558489

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the question of whether AI has rendered self-help nonfiction books obsolete. The original post suggests that AI can easily replace the often superficial content found in self-help books, which many commenters agree with, describing them as "human slop" that is padded to justify their price.

Key points of debate include the value of self-help books versus AI-generated content, with some commenters arguing that AI can distill useful insights from the noise of self-help literature. Others question the actual substance of popular self-help titles, labeling many as scams or overly simplistic. There is also a discussion about the shift of self-help content to modern platforms like podcasts and YouTube, where creators can monetize their content more effectively than through traditional publishing.

Notable insights include the acknowledgment that while self-help books may be declining, the demand for personal development content persists, now taking different forms. Some commenters highlight the importance of visual and audio content for learning, suggesting that the medium of delivery is evolving alongside consumer preferences. Overall, the conversation reflects a broader trend in how people seek and consume self-help and educational material in the digital age.
