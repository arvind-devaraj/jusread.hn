Ask HN: Why are so many "AI evangelists" posting such insufferable content?  (67 pts, 39 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48765450

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a user's frustration with the overwhelming presence of "AI evangelists" on LinkedIn, who frequently post self-promotional content that often lacks substance. The original poster notes that their feed is filled with individuals branding themselves as "AI Thought Leaders" and sharing repetitive, sensationalized claims about AI, often dismissing those who don't engage with these trends as outdated.

In the comments, many users express agreement with the original sentiment, criticizing LinkedIn for fostering a culture of superficial self-promotion and regurgitated content. Some commenters suggest that this trend mirrors past phenomena like cryptocurrency hype, with many participants lacking genuine expertise. Others point out that much of the content is likely AI-generated, contributing to its lack of authenticity. Notably, there’s a consensus that the platform rewards empty platitudes over meaningful contributions, leading to a dilution of valuable discourse.

Several commenters also reflect on the broader implications of this trend, suggesting that the rise of AI content creators may be a response to job insecurity and the pressure to adapt to new technologies. Overall, the discussion highlights a growing discontent with the quality of professional networking on LinkedIn and raises questions about the authenticity of online personas in the age of AI.
