Ask HN: AI productivity gains – do you fire devs or build better products?  (114 pts, 204 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47475859

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the impact of AI on developer productivity and whether companies should reduce their workforce in light of these gains or focus on enhancing product quality. The original poster (OP) shares their experience with AI tools, noting significant productivity improvements in handling boilerplate code, libraries, and refactoring tasks. They express skepticism about the long-term claims of a 90% productivity increase for complex systems but highlight the efficiency gained in simpler tasks, suggesting that companies could leverage this to outperform competitors rather than downsizing.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and skepticism regarding the actual productivity gains from AI. Some commenters argue that while productivity may increase, it doesn't necessarily translate to proportional profit increases, especially in saturated markets. Others caution against overestimating AI's capabilities, emphasizing that it may not replace developers but rather augment their work. Notable insights include concerns about the quality of AI-generated code and the potential for companies to prioritize layoffs over innovation, depending on their market strategies. Overall, the discussion reflects a nuanced view of AI's role in software development, balancing optimism with caution.
