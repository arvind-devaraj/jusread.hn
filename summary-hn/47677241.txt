Taste in the age of AI and LLMs  (265 pts, 204 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47677241

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the implications of AI and large language models (LLMs) on "taste" in creative and technical work. The original post argues that as AI-generated content becomes more prevalent, the value of human-created work may diminish, leading to a shift in how taste is perceived and valued. The author suggests that "taste" could serve as a unique differentiator in a landscape increasingly dominated by AI.

In the comments, there is a mix of skepticism and concern about the future of creative work. Some users argue that the commoditization of taste may lag behind AI advancements, making it a potential "moat" for human creators, while others believe that AI's ability to recognize and generate trends could undermine the uniqueness of human taste. There is also a debate about whether the average consumer prefers lower-quality content, which could impact how AI models are trained and what they produce.

Notable insights include the idea that taste is not just a subjective measure but is influenced by social dynamics and market preferences. Some commenters express frustration with the perceived decline in quality and originality in content, suggesting that the current environment may lead to a homogenization of creative outputs. Overall, the discussion highlights the complex relationship between AI, creativity, and the evolving standards of taste in a rapidly changing technological landscape.
