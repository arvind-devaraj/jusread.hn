Amazon Prime AI overviews can't even get the basics right  (43 pts, 13 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46508324

The Hacker News discussion centers around a user's frustration with the inaccurate AI-generated overviews for the TV show "Frasier" on Amazon Prime. The user points out specific errors, such as misplacing character events and roles, and criticizes Amazon for relying on AI instead of utilizing the accurate summaries available on IMDb, which they own.

In the comments, many users express agreement with the original poster's sentiments, highlighting a broader issue of AI-generated content being unreliable and often nonsensical. Some commenters share their own experiences with AI inaccuracies in product descriptions and recommendations on Amazon, emphasizing a growing fatigue with AI's presence in digital content. Notably, there is a consensus that human-written summaries are preferable and that the reliance on AI for such tasks is unnecessary, especially when accurate information is readily available. Overall, the discussion reflects a critical view of AI's current capabilities in content generation.
