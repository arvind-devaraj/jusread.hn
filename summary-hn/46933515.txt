Show HN: Fine-tuned Qwen2.5-7B on 100 films for probabilistic story graphs  (101 pts, 20 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46933515

The Hacker News discussion centers around a project called CineGraphs, developed by a computer systems engineering student who transitioned from film school. The tool aims to help filmmakers explore narrative possibilities by generating branching story graphs based on user-inputted concepts. Unlike typical AI writing tools that produce generic outputs, CineGraphs is fine-tuned on a curated selection of 100 high-quality films to understand cinematic storytelling better. Users can visualize narrative paths and export them into a screenplay format, allowing for a more exploratory writing process.

In the comments, users express enthusiasm for the tool but also highlight areas for improvement, such as the need for manual editing of branches and the ability to mark endpoints in the narrative. There is a consensus that the eclectic film selection significantly enhances the model's output quality, and several users suggest potential features like an API for integration with other writing systems. Notably, some commenters draw parallels between the tool's approach and creative processes like Brian Eno's Oblique Strategies, emphasizing the importance of generative constraints in fostering creativity. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of excitement about the tool's potential and constructive feedback for its development.
