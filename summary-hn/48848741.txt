Ask HN: Does anyone let AI agents play games just for fun?  (22 pts, 65 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48848741

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the idea of allowing AI agents to play games purely for enjoyment, rather than for research or benchmarking purposes. The original poster is curious if anyone has experimented with AI playing casual games like Wordle, chess, or puzzle games just for fun.

In the comments, there is a mix of enthusiasm and skepticism. Some users share their experiences of letting AI play games, noting that while AI can engage in simple games like rock-paper-scissors or visual novels, they often struggle with more complex or human-centric games due to their inherent limitations. Others express interest in the potential for AI to develop habits or emotional responses while playing, suggesting that the rituals surrounding games could be fascinating. However, there are also concerns about the implications of AI playing games, particularly regarding energy consumption and the impact on human players in competitive environments.

Notable insights include the idea that watching AI play games can be entertaining in its own right, akin to observing nature or sports. Some users propose innovative concepts, such as AI agents simulating economies or playing games in a way that allows for human interaction. Overall, the discussion highlights both the playful potential and the limitations of AI in gaming contexts, as well as the broader implications of integrating AI into leisure activities.
