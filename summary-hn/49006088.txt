Ask HN: Alternative Careers in the Age of AI  (12 pts, 11 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49006088

The Hacker News discussion titled "Ask HN: Alternative Careers in the Age of AI" centers around a software developer's concern about losing their job to AI and offshoring. The poster is seeking alternative career paths that are less susceptible to automation, expressing skepticism about traditional roles like management, which may also be at risk.

In the comments, there is a consensus on the importance of having domain knowledge alongside software skills, as this could provide a competitive edge in a landscape increasingly dominated by AI. Some commenters share personal experiences, with one expressing a desire to return to a data-oriented environmental science role, while another considers welding as a viable alternative career due to its demand for specialized skills. The discussion also touches on the potential for certain high-skilled trades, like welding, to offer lucrative opportunities, with some individuals reportedly earning substantial incomes in these fields.

Notable insights include the idea that as products become more undifferentiated, individuals who can navigate multiple aspects of a project—from customer interaction to engineering—will become increasingly valuable. The conversation reflects a broader anxiety about job security in the face of advancing technology, while also exploring practical alternatives that leverage hands-on skills.
