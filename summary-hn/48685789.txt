What Would C. S. Lewis Have Thought of AI?  (10 pts, 11 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48685789

The Hacker News discussion revolves around an article speculating on what C. S. Lewis might have thought about artificial intelligence (AI). The article references Lewis's works, particularly "The Abolition of Man" and "That Hideous Strength," suggesting that he would have been critical of modern AI developments and the way his name is used in AI-generated content.

In the comments, there is a consensus that Lewis's critiques from decades ago remain relevant today, especially regarding the ethical implications of technology. Some users express admiration for Lewis's insights, while others critique his theological perspectives, arguing they lack depth compared to those of his friend J.R.R. Tolkien. There is also a debate about Lewis's religious affiliation, with clarifications about his Protestant background and his relationship with Catholicism.

Notable insights include a quote from Lewis that emphasizes caution towards entities that are "going to be human" but aren't, reflecting a broader concern about the implications of AI. Additionally, the discussion touches on the complexities of categorizing Lewis's theological stance and the nuances of Anglicanism, highlighting the diverse interpretations of his beliefs.
