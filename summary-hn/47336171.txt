Show HN: Open-source browser for AI agents  (155 pts, 50 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47336171

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the introduction of the agent-browser-protocol (ABP), an open-source browser forked from Chromium designed to enhance interactions between AI agents and web pages. The creator, known as "theredsix," explains that traditional browser-agent failures often stem from the agent operating on outdated page states. ABP addresses this by synchronizing the agent with the browser after each action, freezing execution to capture the current state and notable events, which allows the agent to make informed decisions based on a fresh visual context.

In the comments, users engage in discussions about ABP's features compared to other tools, such as agent-browser.dev, and suggest improvements like allowing agents to select specific portions of a page for context. There is also interest in the potential for ABP to assist with other projects, such as ArchiveBox, due to its ability to pause execution and reduce CPU usage. Notably, the creator acknowledges the need for headless mode support and discusses the challenges of determining when a page is "settled" for reliable interactions.

Overall, the discussion highlights the innovative approach of ABP in improving AI-agent interactions with web content, while also addressing practical concerns and suggestions from the community regarding its functionality and potential applications.
