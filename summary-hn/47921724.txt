Google DeepMind Paper Argues LLMs Will Never Be Conscious  (20 pts, 26 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47921724

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a paper from Google DeepMind that argues large language models (LLMs) will never achieve consciousness. The paper suggests that consciousness is a trait that cannot be transferred from biological entities to machines, emphasizing that if an artificial system were to be conscious, it would depend on its physical constitution rather than its syntactic architecture.

In the comments, there is significant debate about the definition of consciousness and whether it is inherently biological. Some users argue that consciousness might not be limited to biological entities, while others question the relevance of discussing consciousness in the context of text generators like LLMs. A few commenters express skepticism about the paper's conclusions, suggesting that the argument is reductive and overlooks the potential for LLMs to be part of more complex systems that could exhibit consciousness.

Notable insights include the idea that the definition of consciousness is crucial to the discussion, with some commenters advocating for a more rigorous philosophical and scientific exploration of the concept. Others highlight the moral implications of deploying LLMs that simulate human-like behavior, regardless of their lack of genuine consciousness. Overall, the conversation reflects a mix of philosophical inquiry and practical concerns regarding the future of AI.
