Inducing self-NSFW classification in image models to prevent deepfakes edits  (20 pts, 16 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46496357

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a post where the author explores a novel approach to image generation models by inducing them to classify benign images as NSFW (Not Safe For Work) through mild transformations. This method aims to stress-test the models' safety layers rather than bypass them, potentially raising the cost for those who misuse such systems. The author plans to open-source this tool once it becomes more stable.

In the comments, there is a debate about the implications of using such tools. Some users argue that deepfake technology can be both beneficial and harmful, likening the situation to banning tools like knives due to their potential misuse. Others express concerns about the risks of legitimate images being incorrectly flagged as NSFW, which could have serious consequences. Notably, there are discussions about the balance between freedom and safety, with suggestions that social media could require identification to mitigate misuse while preserving user rights.

Overall, the conversation highlights the complexities of moderating AI-generated content, the ethical considerations of tool usage, and the challenges of ensuring safety without stifling creativity or legitimate use.
