AI is a bad tool  (75 pts, 85 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48897861

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the assertion that AI is a "bad tool" for coding, as articulated in a submitted article. The main argument is that relying on AI for code generation can lead to poor quality and unverified outputs, raising concerns about the long-term implications for software development and craftsmanship.

In the comments, there is a lively debate about the effectiveness and reliability of AI in coding. Some users argue that AI tools significantly enhance productivity and can produce quality code, especially for end-user applications and internal tools. Others caution that while AI can be beneficial, it may introduce errors and instability, particularly in larger, more complex codebases. A recurring theme is the distinction between coding for immediate use versus creating robust libraries or tools that require higher reliability.

Notable insights include the idea that while AI can expedite the coding process, it may also diminish the joy and craftsmanship associated with coding. Some commenters express concern that over-reliance on AI could lead to a lack of deep understanding among developers, potentially compromising the quality of future software. Others counter that AI should be viewed as a powerful tool that allows developers to focus more on design and problem-solving rather than the mechanics of coding itself.
