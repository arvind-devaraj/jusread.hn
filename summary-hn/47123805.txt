AIs can generate near-verbatim copies of novels from training data  (86 pts, 106 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47123805

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a recent article that highlights how AI models can generate near-verbatim copies of novels from their training data. This raises significant questions about copyright and the ethical implications of AI-generated content.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and debate regarding the nature of AI language models (LLMs) and their ability to reproduce copyrighted material. Some users argue that since LLMs are essentially prediction machines, they can output sequences that resemble the original texts if prompted correctly. Others counter that while LLMs can generate text similar to their training data, they do not literally memorize or regurgitate it verbatim due to their lossy compression nature. The discussion also delves into legal implications, with users debating whether the act of jailbreaking models to extract copyrighted text constitutes a copyright violation and how fair use might apply in these scenarios.

Notable insights include the complexity of copyright law as it pertains to AI, with some commenters suggesting that the mere existence of copyrighted material within a model's training data complicates the legal landscape. The conversation reflects broader concerns about the responsibilities of AI developers and the potential need for clearer regulations regarding AI-generated content and copyright infringement.
