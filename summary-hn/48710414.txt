Do LLMs pass the mirror test?  (85 pts, 68 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48710414

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the question of whether large language models (LLMs) can pass the mirror test, a measure of self-awareness typically used in animals. The original post discusses how LLMs primarily operate through text and user-assistant interactions, raising questions about their self-awareness and introspection capabilities. 

In the comments, there is significant debate about the nature of reasoning in LLMs. Some users argue that LLMs merely predict the next token based on patterns in their training data, lacking true reasoning or self-awareness. Others contend that the complexity and size of these models may allow for emergent behaviors that resemble reasoning. There are also discussions about the limitations of LLMs, particularly in tasks requiring abstract reasoning or logic, and whether their outputs can be considered genuine reasoning or merely an illusion.

Notable insights include the idea that LLMs might exhibit behaviors that could be interpreted as self-awareness under certain conditions, but fundamentally, they remain sophisticated pattern recognition systems. The conversation highlights the ongoing tension between viewing LLMs as advanced tools versus entities capable of genuine cognitive processes, reflecting broader concerns about anthropomorphizing AI technologies.
