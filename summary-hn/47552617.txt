https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47552617

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the implications of AI on skill development in both adults and children, as highlighted in a Psychology Today article. The post argues that adults are losing essential skills due to reliance on AI, while children may never develop these skills in the first place. 

In the comments, there is a debate about whether AI serves as a helpful educational tool or if it undermines fundamental learning. Some users argue that AI can enhance learning by acting as a tutor, while others contend that it reduces the necessary struggle that fosters deep understanding and skill acquisition. A few commenters draw parallels to the introduction of calculators, questioning whether the ease provided by technology detracts from essential cognitive development.

Notable insights include the idea that while AI can accelerate learning, it may also prevent individuals from developing a strong foundational understanding of subjects. There is also a discussion about the balance between leveraging technology for efficiency and ensuring that individuals engage in the learning process to build lasting skills. Overall, the conversation reflects a mix of optimism and caution regarding the role of AI in education and skill development.
