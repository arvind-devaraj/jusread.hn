OpenAI proposes handing Trump administration 5% stake  (37 pts, 9 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48756702

The Hacker News discussion revolves around OpenAI's proposal to give the Trump administration a 5% equity stake in the company, with the idea that this could help share the financial benefits of AI with the public. The proposal suggests that other leading AI companies might also contribute a similar stake, potentially creating a fund akin to the Alaska Permanent Fund, which invests state resources and pays dividends to residents.

In the comments, there is a mix of support and skepticism. Some users express concerns about cronyism and conflicts of interest, particularly given the current political climate. Others argue that the concept of government ownership in businesses could be a viable way to address taxation and national security. A few commenters highlight the potential for this arrangement to benefit the public rather than just the government, suggesting that it could provide ongoing advantages even after the current administration.

Notable insights include the recognition of the complexities and potential pitfalls of such a proposal, with some commenters questioning the motivations behind it and the implications for democracy and corporate governance. Overall, the discussion reflects a broader debate about the intersection of technology, government, and public welfare.
