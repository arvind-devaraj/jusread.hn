Show HN: I built a tool to assist AI agents to know when a PR is good to go  (45 pts, 35 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46656759

The Hacker News discussion centers around a tool called "Good To Go" (gtg), created by the user dsifry to help AI agents determine when a pull request (PR) is ready to merge. The tool addresses common issues faced by AI agents, such as missing actionable comments during code reviews and not having a clear, deterministic method to assess PR readiness. gtg aggregates continuous integration (CI) status, classifies review comments, and tracks thread resolutions, providing a straightforward output indicating whether a PR is ready.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and concern regarding the implications of automating the PR readiness process. Some users appreciate the tool's ability to improve workflow and code quality, while others worry about the potential for AI to bypass human review entirely. There are discussions about the balance between productivity and the risks of relying too heavily on AI, with some commenters advocating for maintaining human oversight in the review process. Notably, dsifry emphasizes that the tool does not automatically merge PRs, which he views as a dangerous practice, and instead focuses on ensuring all actionable comments are addressed before human review.

Overall, the conversation highlights the evolving role of AI in software development, the importance of maintaining quality control, and the need for clear boundaries between automated processes and human judgment.
