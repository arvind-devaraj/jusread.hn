A.I. note takers are making lawyers nervous  (264 pts, 192 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48093043

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a New York Times article discussing the concerns lawyers have regarding AI note-taking tools. These tools, while seen as useful for capturing meeting details, raise significant issues, particularly around the potential to compromise attorney-client privilege. The main worry is that AI-generated transcripts may inadvertently include sensitive information or context that could be accessible to outside parties, thus waiving this privilege.

In the comments, there is a consensus on the risks associated with AI note-takers, particularly their inaccuracies and the potential for misinterpretation of context. Many commenters express skepticism about the reliability of AI in capturing nuanced conversations, emphasizing that AI often fails to recognize tone and body language, which can lead to misleading records. Some also highlight the broader implications of turning casual discussions into permanent records that could be used against individuals in legal contexts.

Notable insights include the idea that the technology should maintain attorney-client privilege regardless of the medium used, and the suggestion that AI's tendency to generate confident but incorrect outputs could be mitigated by better training to indicate uncertainty. Overall, the discussion reflects a cautious approach to adopting AI in sensitive fields like law, where accuracy and confidentiality are paramount.
