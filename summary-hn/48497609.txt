If you are asking for human attention, demonstrate human effort  (1753 pts, 501 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48497609

The Hacker News discussion titled "If you are asking for human attention, demonstrate human effort" revolves around the increasing reliance on AI-generated content in professional communication and the implications it has for quality and accountability. The original post suggests that if individuals expect others to engage with their work, they should demonstrate genuine human effort in its creation.

In the comments, there is a lively debate about the quality of AI-generated content. Some users argue that AI often produces verbose and low-quality output, leading to a preference for brevity and clarity in communication. Others express frustration with colleagues who rely heavily on AI without adding their own insights, resulting in unreviewed and nonsensical documents. There is a consensus that while AI can be a useful tool, it should not replace critical thinking and personal effort in communication.

Notable insights include the idea that the expectation of accountability should come with a demonstration of effort, and that the quality of communication is more important than the method of its production. Several commenters emphasize the need for individuals to take ownership of their work, regardless of whether AI was involved, and to ensure that their contributions are meaningful and well-considered.
