Make Trust Irrelevant: A Gamer's Take on Agentic AI Safety  (10 pts, 10 comments)

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a position paper that critiques current approaches to agentic AI safety, arguing that they often repeat the "confused deputy problem." The author, DesoPK, contends that AI agents are given too much authority without adequate hard constraints, suggesting that true safety requires enforceable boundaries rather than soft constraints like prompts or userland wrappers.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and skepticism. Some users question whether the paper was generated by a language model and suggest transparency about its creation. Others highlight the tension between user convenience and security, with DesoPK acknowledging that while user experience is crucial, authority must be tightly controlled to prevent misuse. Notably, a comparison is made between AI agents and players in competitive games, emphasizing that while game players operate within fixed rules, AI agents often have access to sensitive information, complicating safety measures.

Overall, the discussion underscores the need for robust safety mechanisms in AI systems while grappling with the challenges of maintaining user-friendly experiences.
