More than 6 out of 10 people turn to AI for psychological support  (83 pts, 88 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48377854

The Hacker News discussion centers around a report indicating that over 60% of people seek psychological support from AI, with 42% often following its advice. Participants in the discussion explore the effectiveness of AI in mental health support compared to traditional therapy, acknowledging that while AI cannot replace genuine human interaction, it can serve as a useful tool for processing thoughts and emotions, similar to journaling.

Key points of agreement include the notion that AI can help individuals articulate their feelings and navigate challenges, though it cannot "fix" problems like a trained therapist can. Some commenters argue that therapy's goal is not necessarily to cure but to help manage mental health issues. However, there is significant debate regarding the efficacy of therapy itself, with some participants questioning its empirical basis and suggesting that many mental health disorders are socially constructed.

Notable insights include the idea that AI can act as a modern substitute for traditional forms of support, like community or social structures, and that it may provide a more accessible and less judgmental space for individuals to express their thoughts. Some commenters express skepticism about the survey's methodology and the potential biases in AI-generated advice, emphasizing the importance of critical thinking when using such tools. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of optimism about AI's role in mental health and caution regarding its limitations and the complexities of human psychology.
