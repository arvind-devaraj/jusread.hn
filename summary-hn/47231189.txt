Legal AI slop is becoming a real problem  (28 pts, 11 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47231189

The Hacker News discussion revolves around concerns regarding the use of generative AI in legal documents, particularly highlighted by a case where a landlord's lawyers submitted a brief containing "hallucinatory" citations generated by AI. This incident raises alarms about the reliability of AI in legal contexts and the potential consequences of its misuse.

Commenters express a mix of disbelief and concern about the implications of AI-generated content in legal proceedings. Some highlight the inherent unreliability of AI, suggesting that lawyers may soon face liability issues as they increasingly rely on these tools. Others share personal experiences, with one user noting their own use of AI in legal matters while emphasizing the importance of thorough research and verification. There is a general consensus that while AI can assist in legal tasks, its current limitations pose significant risks, and the legal profession may need to adapt to these challenges.
