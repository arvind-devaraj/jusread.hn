Show HN: I run a vision model on every screenshot, locally, on a 4GB GPU  (36 pts, 7 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48522417

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a project called ScreenMind, which allows users to run a vision model on every screenshot locally using a 4GB GPU. The creator emphasizes that the tool operates entirely on-device, ensuring that no data is sent to the cloud, addressing privacy concerns associated with similar applications.

In the comments, there is a consensus that the backlash against Microsoft's cloud-based screen-aware AI, Recall, stemmed from privacy issues rather than the concept itself. Users express appreciation for ScreenMind's local, opt-in approach, which alleviates fears about data security. One commenter shares their positive experience with a similar app, Rewind, highlighting the value of local data processing. Additionally, there is a discussion about the limitations of certain AI models, with the creator explaining how they optimized their approach by using narrow tasks and structured prompts to achieve better results.

Overall, the conversation underscores the importance of privacy in AI applications and the potential for local processing to enhance user trust and functionality.
