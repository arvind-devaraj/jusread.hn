Preliminary data from a longitudinal AI impact study  (62 pts, 40 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47342139

The Hacker News discussion revolves around preliminary findings from a longitudinal study on the impact of AI on productivity, which suggests an approximate 10% increase in productivity across various industries. Commenters reflect on their experiences with AI tools, particularly highlighting the improvements seen with recent model releases like Opus 4.5, which many believe have significantly enhanced their capabilities.

Key points of agreement include the notion that while AI tools are improving, the human processes surrounding their use may still be bottlenecks in productivity. Some commenters argue that the 10% gain, while notable, might not be as impressive given the substantial investments made in AI technology. Others emphasize that the true value of AI lies in its ability to automate routine tasks, allowing developers to focus on more complex issues, though this also raises concerns about the potential for increased complexity and mistakes in software development.

Notable insights include the idea that AI acts as a "force multiplier," significantly enhancing the productivity of skilled developers while potentially diminishing the roles of junior developers. There is also a recognition that while current gains may seem modest, there is still potential for further improvements as AI technology evolves. Overall, the discussion reflects a mix of optimism and caution regarding the future of AI in the workplace.
