Using Chinese AI models to get a degree  (20 pts, 8 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48139058

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the use of Chinese AI models, specifically Qwen and GLM, to assist in obtaining academic degrees. The original post highlights a personal experience where the author faced accusations of academic dishonesty due to results generated by Qwen, which they attributed to its ability to aggregate and synthesize information from various sources.

In the comments, there is a consensus that using these AI models is a legitimate strategy for students, with some arguing that the primary value of a college degree is the credential itself rather than the education received. Several commenters praise the capabilities of Chinese AI models, noting their open-source nature and lack of ethical constraints compared to Western counterparts, which they view as overly cautious. There is also a discussion about the long-term implications of Chinese AI development, suggesting that it may outlast Western models due to its funding structure and open-source availability.

Overall, the conversation reflects a growing trend among students to leverage AI for academic purposes, raising questions about the future of education and the value of traditional degrees in a rapidly changing technological landscape.
