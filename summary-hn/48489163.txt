Show HN: Fata – Spaced repetition to fight skill rot from AI coding  (124 pts, 53 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48489163

The Hacker News discussion centers around a new tool called Fata, created by Djoumé, aimed at combating "skill rot" among developers who increasingly rely on AI for coding. Fata offers short, daily spaced-repetition sessions to help users maintain and enhance their programming skills in various languages and technologies. The app is designed to be accessible in the browser and as a mobile app, with most content generated by AI after thorough testing and review.

In the comments, users express a mix of support and skepticism. Some appreciate the concept of a coding app that encourages regular practice, while others question the app's pricing model and its differentiation from existing platforms like Codecademy. A significant debate arises around the necessity of memorization and repetition in programming, with some arguing that these methods are essential for skill retention, while others believe that practical experience and exposure are more valuable. Notably, some commenters share personal experiences with "skill rot," suggesting that the phenomenon may be overstated, as many find it relatively easy to reacquire skills after a break. Overall, the discussion reflects a broader concern about the impact of AI on developers' technical depth and the future of programming education.
