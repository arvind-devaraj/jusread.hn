The Hacker News discussion revolves around the announcement of Gemini Embedding 2, a new multimodal embedding model developed by Google that can create embeddings for various formats, including video, audio, and documents. Users express excitement about its capabilities, noting that it represents a significant advancement, although some mention that the context size is still smaller compared to traditional text embeddings.

In the comments, there is a mix of curiosity and comparison with other models. One user inquires about pricing and how Gemini Embedding 2 stacks up against zembed-1, while another provides pricing details, indicating it costs 20 cents per million tokens for text and 0.012 cents per image. A notable point of debate arises when a user compares Gemini to Qwen's open weight multimodal embedding model, suggesting that Qwen offers superior embeddings and more control through steerability, which they find particularly impressive.

Overall, the discussion highlights enthusiasm for Gemini Embedding 2's capabilities but also raises questions about its pricing and effectiveness compared to existing models, with some users expressing a preference for alternatives that offer more control and better performance.
