Tell HN: Tired of Generic Long Form A.I Posts  (43 pts, 24 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47303755

The Hacker News discussion centers around a user's frustration with the prevalence of generic, AI-generated long-form content that lacks individuality and authenticity. The original poster expresses a desire for more genuine writing and criticizes those who produce what they call "social media slop" for platforms like LinkedIn and Hacker News.

In the comments, several users agree with the sentiment, sharing their experiences and preferences for authentic writing over polished but soulless AI-generated text. Some commenters mention that they prefer to see posts with grammatical errors, as they feel it reflects a real person's voice. Others highlight specific characteristics of AI-generated content, such as overused phrases and formatting styles that lack originality. There is also a discussion about the importance of maintaining a personal touch in communication, even in professional settings.

Notable insights include the idea that authenticity may become more valued than grammatical correctness, and the suggestion that authors could label their use of AI in content creation to clarify its origin. Overall, the conversation reflects a broader concern about the impact of AI on writing quality and the loss of individual expression in digital communication.
