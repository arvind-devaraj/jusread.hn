OpenAI to Take a Percentage from Customer AI-Assisted R&D Outcomes  (22 pts, 25 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46739278

The Hacker News discussion revolves around OpenAI's potential decision to take a percentage of profits from AI-assisted research and development outcomes generated by its customers. This proposal has sparked significant debate regarding its implications for industries reliant on intellectual property (IP), such as pharmaceuticals and technology.

Commenters express a range of opinions. Some argue that this approach could deter established companies from adopting OpenAI's technology, while others believe that startups with less to lose will embrace the opportunity, ensuring continued industry advancement. There are concerns about the credibility of the claims, with some users questioning the sources of information and the feasibility of enforcing such a profit-sharing model. Notably, some commenters liken OpenAI's proposal to an "idea guy" demanding a cut after others do the work, suggesting a potential backlash against the model.

Overall, the discussion highlights a tension between innovation and IP rights, with many users predicting that if OpenAI implements this model, it could lead to significant pushback from businesses that rely on proprietary developments.
