Letting AI play my game – building an agentic test harness to help play-testing  (136 pts, 36 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47947525

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a blog post by Jeff Schomay, detailing his experience creating an AI-driven test harness to play-test his game. The harness utilizes a text-based representation of the game, allowing the AI to analyze game mechanics and identify bugs or balance issues. Schomay shares insights on how this approach has improved his play-testing process, enabling the AI to validate features and discover unexpected bugs.

In the comments, several users agree on the effectiveness of using AI for game testing, sharing their own experiences with similar projects. Key points of discussion include the importance of combining source code analysis with live testing for better results, the potential for AI to assist in balancing games, and the challenges of implementing AI in real-time games. Notably, some commenters express skepticism about the idea of AI-driven play-testing, arguing that it may lack the human touch necessary for creating enjoyable gaming experiences. Others highlight the innovative possibilities AI brings to game development, suggesting that it could lead to more tightly balanced and engaging games.
