Ask HN: What's the current best local/open speech-to-speech setup?  (265 pts, 71 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46731068

The Hacker News discussion revolves around finding the best local and open speech-to-speech setups for voice assistants, focusing on low latency and real-time performance. The original poster expresses frustration at the lack of comprehensive guides for achieving a fully local speech-to-speech system, particularly with the Qwen3 Omni model, and seeks recommendations for effective combinations of tools and hardware that work well together.

In the comments, users share various tools and models they have experimented with, such as Whisper, Parakeet, and Nvidia's PersonaPlex, highlighting their experiences with local setups. There is a consensus that while end-to-end speech models are promising, many users still rely on a pipeline approach that involves separate speech-to-text (ASR), language models (LLM), and text-to-speech (TTS) components. Some commenters note the challenges of achieving real-time performance and the need for tuning and configuration.

Notable insights include the recognition that the current state of speech-to-speech technology often requires a combination of models rather than a single solution, with some users advocating for the flexibility of using tools like pipecat to integrate various components. Additionally, there is a discussion about the importance of maintaining a text layer for complex tasks, suggesting that fully end-to-end models may not yet be ideal for all applications.
