Predicting OpenAI's ad strategy  (584 pts, 532 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46668021

The Hacker News discussion revolves around the potential advertising strategy of OpenAI, particularly in relation to its AI products like ChatGPT. The original post speculates on how ads might be integrated into these platforms, raising concerns about the implications for user experience and the nature of AI-generated content.

In the comments, there is a mix of agreement and debate regarding the inevitability of ads appearing across all tiers of OpenAI's offerings, even in premium subscriptions. Some users express skepticism about the effectiveness of such ads, while others suggest that they could be subtly woven into responses, akin to product placements in media. A notable point of contention is whether users would accept being double-billed for both premium services and ads, with some arguing that consumers are already accustomed to this model in other contexts, like cable TV.

Several commenters highlight the potential for AI to influence consumer behavior through subtle recommendations, raising ethical questions about transparency and the integrity of AI outputs. Others advocate for a disconnect from ad-driven platforms altogether, suggesting that user disengagement might be the only way to push back against pervasive advertising practices. Overall, the discussion reflects a broader concern about the commercialization of AI and its impact on user autonomy and trust.
