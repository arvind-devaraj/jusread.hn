NanoGPT Slowrun: Language Modeling with Limited Data, Infinite Compute  (185 pts, 32 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47251259

The Hacker News discussion centers around the "NanoGPT Slowrun" project, which explores language modeling using limited data while leveraging abundant computational resources. The project aims to improve data efficiency in training language models, contrasting with traditional approaches that assume unlimited data but limited compute. The authors plan to incorporate insights from related challenges, such as the BabyLM challenge, while emphasizing their unique focus on maximizing data efficiency through increased compute.

In the comments, there is a debate about the project's methodology, particularly its choice of baseline models and the risk of overfitting due to the lack of a validation set. Some commenters express concerns about the potential for memorization rather than generalization, while others highlight the innovative approach of flipping the typical constraints in machine learning. There is also discussion about the implications of using second-order optimizers and ensemble methods for improving data efficiency.

Notable insights include the recognition that current models are far from achieving human-like sample efficiency, suggesting significant room for improvement. Commenters also emphasize the importance of tracking overfitting and the potential for generating additional training data through self-generated tasks, indicating a forward-thinking approach to enhancing model performance.
