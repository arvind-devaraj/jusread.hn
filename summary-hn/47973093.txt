Show HN: Loopsy, a way for terminals and AI agents on different machines to talk  (58 pts, 12 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47973093

The Hacker News discussion revolves around "Loopsy," a tool created by the author to enable communication between terminals and AI agents on different machines, particularly for file transfers and command execution. The author initially aimed to optimize resource use between two MacBooks and later expanded the functionality to allow remote access to coding sessions via a Cloudflare worker. They acknowledge the potential for reinventing existing solutions but express satisfaction with the tool's effectiveness and mention ongoing work on end-to-end encryption.

In the comments, users generally appreciate the practicality of Loopsy, highlighting how it allows for seamless integration of multiple machines and agents. There is a discussion about the tool's reliance on Cloudflare and the possibility of self-hosting it. Some commenters compare Loopsy to existing solutions like Tailscale, noting its unique features, such as the ability to control agents from a phone and facilitate communication between them. However, concerns are raised about file management and concurrency issues, with suggestions for implementing file locking mechanisms to prevent conflicts during simultaneous edits. Overall, the conversation emphasizes the innovative potential of Loopsy while recognizing areas for improvement.
