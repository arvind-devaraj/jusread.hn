Google and Pentagon reportedly agree on deal for 'any lawful' use of AI  (317 pts, 269 comments)
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47936156

The Hacker News discussion revolves around a reported agreement between Google and the Pentagon for the use of AI in "any lawful" manner. The deal raises concerns about the definition of "lawful" and who determines it, especially given the classified nature of the agreement. Many commenters express skepticism about the government's ability to adhere to legal standards, fearing that the executive branch could interpret laws creatively to justify unlawful actions.

Key points of debate include the lack of oversight in how the government might use AI, the implications of private corporations influencing government actions, and the potential for overclassification to hinder public accountability. Some users argue that Google should not dictate what is lawful, while others highlight the historical precedent of government agencies operating with minimal checks. Notable insights include the comparison of this situation to past instances of corporate-government collusion and the broader implications for democracy and accountability in the face of rapidly advancing technology.
